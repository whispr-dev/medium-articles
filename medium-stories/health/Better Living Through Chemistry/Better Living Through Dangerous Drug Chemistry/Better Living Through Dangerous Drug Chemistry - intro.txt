Better Living Through Dangerous Drugs Chemistry

by Claudia G. Petersen

We live in a society that consumes drugs constantly, passionately, and often desperately — while insisting it hates them.

Coffee to wake up.
Alcohol to come down.
Nicotine to focus.
SSRIs to cope.
Painkillers to function.

And then we draw an arbitrary moral line and pretend the rest are “dangerous.”

This is not a chemistry problem.
This is a vibes problem.

Chemistry, inconveniently, does not care which substances we’ve decided are respectable. Molecules do not know whether they were prescribed, illegal, artisanal, or sold in pastel packaging by a wellness brand.

They bind where they bind.
They act where they act.
They do what they do.

In this series, we are not going to debate whether drugs are “good” or “bad.” We are going to do something far more threatening:

We are going to understand them.

We’ll talk about why some substances feel miraculous at low doses and catastrophic at high ones. Why addiction is not a moral failure but a predictable outcome of certain molecular properties. Why the drugs we fear most are often the ones we understand least.

This is not encouragement.
This is not endorsement.
This is literacy.

Because the most dangerous thing about drugs has never been the chemistry.

It’s the mythology.
